About Me

I'm a doctoral researcher at the ETH AI Center, collaborating with Andreas Krause and Melanie Zeilinger.

Research Methodology

My work addresses sequential decision-making under complex objectives, including non-Markovian rewards and convex functionals. These theoretically challenging problems—often NP-Hard to approximate—find applications in experiment design, informative path planning, and molecular optimization.

Safety & Guarantees

I develop practical algorithms with conditional provable guarantees, emphasizing safety-critical applications for autonomous systems operating in uncertain environments with a-priori unknown constraints.

Research Areas

My work spans reinforcement learning, control theory, and AI safety

Submodular Reinforcement Learning

Bridging submodular optimization with RL to handle non-additive reward structures in experiment design and informative path planning.

Safe Multi-Agent Learning
