About Me

I'm a doctoral researcher at the ETH AI Center, where I collaborate with Andreas Krause and Melanie Zeilinger.

My research focuses on sequential decision making under complex objectives, including non-Markovian rewards, convex functionals, and non-additive reinforcement learning. While these problems are theoretically challenging—often NP-Hard even to approximate—they have significant applications in experiment design, informative path planning, and molecule design.

I develop practical algorithms with conditional provable guarantees, with a strong emphasis on safety aspects for autonomous execution in unpredictable environments.
